XR Campus Tour

An interactive extended reality campus guide that combines location-based technology, augmented reality, and a virtual guide to explore the university environment in an immersive way.

Explore the Project

About This Portfolio

This website documents the full development process of the XR Campus Tour project. Instead of only presenting the final outcome, this portfolio focuses on the design process, research, experimentation, and iterative development.

The goal is to demonstrate how the idea evolved from an initial concept into a functional XR experience.

Project Overview

The project aims to enhance traditional campus visits by introducing interactive digital content. When users approach landmarks, an AR character appears to introduce the location through narration and visual interaction.

Key Concepts

📍

Location-Based

Detects geographic position and triggers interactive XR content when users approach predefined landmarks.

🤖

AR Guide

A virtual character appears through AR to introduce campus locations with narration and visual cues.

✨

Immersive

Creates a story-driven tour where users explore the campus through interactive digital experiences.

Design Process

1

Initial Concept

The project began with the idea of improving campus tour experiences for visitors where traditional maps or human guides are unavailable.

2

Research

Focused on XR technologies and existing digital tour applications to identify how spatial storytelling can enhance campus environments.

3

Interaction Design

Explored interaction models: how users trigger content, how the AR guide appears, and maintaining engagement without being overwhelming.

4

Prototype Development

Implemented location detection and AR visualization using XR tools, creating a basic virtual guide character.

5

Testing & Refinement

Evaluated and refined trigger distances and narration flow to make the experience smoother for users.

"Reflecting on the journey..."

Developing the XR Campus Tour project highlighted the importance of integrating physical environments with digital interaction. Designing experiences that respond to real-world locations requires careful consideration of both technical constraints and user experience.
